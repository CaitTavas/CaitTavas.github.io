Using a CNN to Predict Positivity in IMDB Reviews - Data Analytics for InfoSci, INFO 3950

Skills and Concepts: Creating and training NN in Python using PyTorch, also utilized numpy

Summary: More of an exploratory final project, we were building up to the usage of creating our own neural networks in Python. Choosing the IMDB data, where the input was a text review and the assigned label was 0 or 1 for positive, I utilized backpropogation, dropouts, and different outputs to create different NN to compare to a logistic regression baseline. Only running on 20 epochs as any further would be overfitting, I found a model that produced a 98% accuracy score on the training, and 84% on the test. The CNN turned out to not be the best model; and performed worse on the training set.

Examining Impact of Seneca Falls Convention - Text Mining for Hist and Lit, INFO 3350

Skills and Concepts: NLP in Python using SpaCy

Summary: For a final project in my Text Mining for History and Literature class, we were given free reign to pick a point of interest given a historical American corpus between the early 1700s and late 1800s. I isolated my interest to women's history and the Seneca Falls Convention, and decided to focus on the impact of social events on the general corpus. I first researched the Seneca Falls itself, and using course concepts I created word embeddings in the SpaCy Python package for the corpus before and after the convention for both male and female authors to create topic models and vector similarities. Unfortunately, there was no statistcal significance in the vector similarities themselves, but there was a clear change in the t-test values.
